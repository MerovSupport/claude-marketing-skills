# Email Copy Principles

## The Moubeche Copy DNA

### Rule 0: The Garlic and Ham Test [Moubeche principle]

Before writing ANY email, ask: **Am I selling what they WANT or what they NEED?**

> "Il faut d'abord donner aux gens ce qu'ils veulent pour ensuite leur donner ce dont ils ont besoin."

Guillaume's mistake at Lemlist: talking about SPF/DKIM algorithms (garlic = what they need). Nobody cared. His prospects wanted: "more replies, more meetings, more revenue" (ham = what they want).

**The garlic** = your technical features, your methodology, your innovation
**The ham** = the outcome they desire, in their words

| ❌ Garlic (what they need) | ✅ Ham (what they want) |
|---------------------------|----------------------|
| "AI-powered sending algorithm" | "Get more replies to your emails" |
| "Multi-channel automation platform" | "Book meetings with qualified prospects" |
| "Advanced segmentation engine" | "Stop wasting time on leads that don't convert" |
| "SPF/DKIM/DMARC optimization" | "Make sure your emails land in the inbox, not spam" |

**Framework: Desire → Trust → Solution**
1. Speak to what they WANT (ham) → gets attention
2. Build trust through the conversation → earns the right to educate
3. THEN give them what they NEED (garlic wrapped in ham)

### Rule 1: Sell the Next Step Only [Moubeche principle]

> "Tu n'arriveras jamais à vendre de façon totalement automatisée. Le but c'est de construire des relations."

Every email has ONE job. Never skip trust levels.

| Email Type | Trust Level | What You're Selling |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Cold outreach | Zero trust | A reply (conversation) |
| Follow-up | Low trust | A meeting / call |
| Warm intro | Medium trust | A demo / trial |
| Nurture | Building trust | A click (content, resource) |
| Sales | High trust | A purchase / upgrade |
| Retention | Established trust | Continued engagement |

### Rule 2: The Tiramisu Technique + RELEVANCE [Moubeche principle]

Layer your email like tiramisu — but every layer must CONNECT to why you're writing.

**Layer 1 (Personalization):** About the PROSPECT. Based on real research — their content, achievements, company news, hiring signals.

**Layer 2 (Relevance Bridge):** Connect that personalization to the problem you solve or value you bring.

**Layer 3 (Value Proposition):** What you can do for them, framed as what they WANT.

**THE RELEVANCE RULE:**

> "Les gens oublient souvent la RELEVANCE. Personnaliser c'est bien, mais il faut que ça ait un lien avec ce que tu vends."

| ❌ Personalized but NOT relevant | ✅ Personalized AND relevant |
|--------------------------------|----------------------------|
| "J'ai vu que tu venais de Paris, je supporte le PSG" | "J'ai vu que tu recrutais 3 SDRs — tu dois scaler ton outbound" |
| "J'adore tes photos de vacances sur Insta" | "J'ai vu ton post sur la croissance de ta boîte — j'ai adoré quand tu parlais de tes erreurs en recrutement" |
| "Belle photo de profil !" | "J'ai vu que tu vendais des formations + tu as une chaîne YouTube avec beaucoup d'abonnés" |

**The test:** Can you draw a straight line from your icebreaker → through their problem → to your value proposition? If not, rewrite the icebreaker.

### Rule 3: PAS Framework as Default [Moubeche principle]

**P**roblem — Name the specific problem they face (in THEIR language)
**A**gitate — Show the impact/cost of not solving it (general, not assuming)
**S**olution — Present your solution as the logical next step

**⚠️ Warning from Guillaume:** "Les mails en mode 'vous êtes CEO, vous devez sûrement vous demander si vous allez pouvoir payer vos employés' — arrêtez." Don't assume specific problems. Speak to general pain points of the role.

Example:
```
[Personalized intro with relevance bridge]

[Problem] Most B2B teams lose 40% of demo requests because follow-up takes 24+ hours.
[Agitate] That's pipeline you already paid to generate — gone.
[Solution] We helped [similar company] cut response time to 5 minutes.

Worth a 10-min chat Thursday or Friday?
```

### Rule 4: 120 Words = Peak Performance [Moubeche principle]
- Cold emails: 50-125 words (120 words = 52% booking rate)
- Nurture emails: 150-300 words max
- Newsletter/educational: 300-500 words max
- ~20 lines including spaces
- 3-5 sentences in the body
- 3rd-5th grade reading level

### Rule 5: Single CTA with Limited Choices [Moubeche principle]

> "C'est comme au resto. Si tu as une carte de 40 pages, tu mets beaucoup de temps à choisir. Si tu as le choix entre deux plats, c'est beaucoup plus simple."

**CTA formats ranked by effectiveness:**

| Format | Example | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Two-option time slot | "Thursday 2pm or Friday 11am?" | Meeting booking (best performer) |
| Yes/no question | "Interested?" | Permission to continue |
| Permission-based | "Can I send you a quick overview?" | Soft intro |
| Direct calendar | "Here's my calendar: [link]" | Warm prospects |
| Value-trade | "I noted 5 ideas for [company]. Want the other 3?" | Cold outreach |
| Simple connection | "Peut-on rentrer en contact ?" | First touch |

**CTA rules:**
- Under 7 words
- Match trust level (cold = small ask, warm = bigger ask)
- Casual language ("chat" > "meeting", "quick call" > "schedule a demo")
- Never: "Book a demo" in a cold email

### Rule 6: Pattern Interrupt [Moubeche principle]

> "La plupart des mails commencent par 'Hello prénom'. Il faut casser ce pattern."

- Don't start with "Bonjour [prénom]"
- Start with "J'ai vu que..." or a personalized observation
- Insert the name in the SECOND sentence only
- This breaks the automated email pattern → feels human

**Before:**
```
Bonjour Jean, je m'appelle Guillaume et je travaille chez...
```

**After:**
```
J'ai vu que votre équipe venait de recruter 3 SDRs —
Jean, ça veut dire que le scale de l'outbound est en cours.
```

### Rule 7: "You" > "I" [Moubeche principle]

> "La plupart des gens font l'erreur de parler d'eux. Le premier message doit être à propos de la personne que tu contactes."

Count your "you/your" vs "I/we/our" ratio. You should win by at least 2:1.

### Rule 8: Each Follow-Up Adds New Value [Moubeche principle]

Never "just checking in." Every follow-up must introduce:
- A new angle on the problem
- A case study or proof point (success stories from similar clients)
- A relevant resource
- A different framing of the value proposition

**Exception:** The very short follow-up works IF the previous email was strong.
> "Les relances très courtes fonctionnent extrêmement bien : 'Bonjour prénom, avez-vous reçu mon mail ?' — taux de réponse aberrant. MAIS si le mail d'avant est un mail de merde, là c'est abuser."

### Rule 9: The Breakup Email [Moubeche principle]

The final email generates significant reply spikes. Structure:
- Acknowledge they're busy
- Summarize your value in one sentence
- Ask if they're the right person (referral question)
- Give permission to say no

**But:** "Ça se teste. J'en ai fait qui ont très bien marché, j'en ai fait qui ont jamais marché et où je me faisais insulter."

### Rule 10: Always Ask for Referrals [Moubeche principle]

Even when the person says NO:

> "Tu te rends compte que ça fit pas. Les gens s'arrêtent là. Non ! Est-ce que tu connais d'autres personnes qui ont le problème XYZ ?"

Template:
```
"Est-ce que tu connais d'autres personnes qui [ont ce problème] ?
Si oui, je te prépare un message et tu leur envoies en me mettant en copie."
```

Make it EASY for them. Prepare the message. They just forward.

### Rule 11: Use Their "Verbe Intime" [Moubeche principle]

Speak in the language your prospects actually use.

> "Quand j'ai des gens qui me prospectent et qui me disent 'vous', je sais que c'est mort."

How to find their language:
- ChatGPT: "Tu es un [job title]. Quels sont les 10 problèmes que tu rencontres le plus souvent ?"
- Client reviews and testimonials (exact words)
- Conversations with existing clients
- Their LinkedIn posts and comments
- Their content (articles, podcasts, videos)

## Copy Frameworks Library

### Framework 1: PAS (Default) [Moubeche principle]
```
[Tiramisu personalization with relevance bridge]

[Problem they likely face — in their language]
[Agitation — cost of inaction]

[Your solution — one sentence, framed as what they WANT]
[Social proof — one line]

[Single CTA with limited choices]
```

### Framework 2: Problem-Impact-Next Step (PIN) [Moubeche principle]
```
Teams struggle with {problem} once they hit {trigger}.
It shows up as {impact metric}.
Curious — are you optimizing for {option A} or {option B}?
```

### Framework 3: Proof-First [Moubeche principle]
```
We helped a {industry} team cut {metric} by {result}.
If {company} is working on something similar,
I can share the 3 changes that made the difference.
```

### Framework 4: Value-First / Ideas Approach [Moubeche principle]
```
[Personalized observation about their business]

I noted 5-6 ideas that could help with [specific area].
Here are the first 3:
1. [idea]
2. [idea]
3. [idea]

If you want the rest, happy to share on a quick 10-min call.
```

This works because you lead with VALUE. The curiosity gap drives the meeting.

### Framework 5: CPPC (Complementary framework, not Moubeche-original)
```
[Contexte — why you're reaching out, how you found them]
[Problème — connect to a problem they face, keep it general]
[Proposition — your solution in one sentence]
[Call to action — simple, e.g., "Peut-on rentrer en contact ?"]
```

### Framework 6: AIDA (for warm/newsletter) [Moubeche principle]
```
[Attention — captivating fact or story]
[Interest — connect to reader's goals]
[Desire — real success story]
[Action — clear implementation step]
```

### Framework 7: Before-After-Bridge (BAB) [Inferred extension]
```
[Before — current painful state]
[After — desired improved state]
[Bridge — how to get there (your solution)]
```

## Tone & Voice Rules

### Cold Outreach Tone:
- Conversational, not corporate
- Direct, not pushy
- Confident, not desperate
- Helpful, not salesy
- Concise, not verbose
- Match the prospect's register (tu vs vous, casual vs formal)

### What to Avoid:
- Buzzwords: "synergy", "leverage", "innovative solution"
- Filler phrases: "I hope this email finds you well"
- "Ça va ?" as an opener — "Inutile de demander comment ça va"
- Guilt trips: "I've sent you 3 emails"
- Feature dumps: listing features without connecting to pain
- Weak qualifiers: "I think maybe", "I was wondering if perhaps"
- Exclamation marks: 0-1 max per email
- Emojis: reduce reply rates by 13% on average [Moubeche data]
- Assuming specific problems: "Vous devez vous demander si vous allez pouvoir payer vos employés"

### Personalization Variables [Moubeche principle]
Beyond {{firstName}} and {{companyName}}, use:
- {{painPoint}} — their specific challenge (from research)
- {{competitor}} — competitor reference
- {{trigger}} — recent event (funding, hiring, product launch)
- {{commonGround}} — shared professional interests (NOT random personal stuff)
- {{metric}} — relevant business metric
- {{icebreaker}} — contextual opener that CONNECTS to your value proposition

Best practice: 3-4 well-placed variables per email. Too many feels robotic.

Make money | LIMITED TIME! | Act now | Call now | You're a winner
Not spam | Get paid | No cost | Work from home | Get started free

## Email Anatomy Checklist

Before sending ANY email, verify:
- [ ] **Garlic & Ham test**: Am I selling what they WANT?
- [ ] **Relevance test**: Does my personalization connect to my value proposition?
- [ ] **#1 Filter**: What does the person GAIN from responding?
- [ ] Subject line: 1-8 words, casual, relevant
- [ ] Opening: about the prospect, not about you. Pattern interrupt (no "Bonjour prénom").
- [ ] Body: 50-125 words (cold) or appropriate length for type
- [ ] "You/your" ratio: at least 2:1 vs "I/we/our"
- [ ] Framework applied: PAS, PIN, Proof-First, or appropriate
- [ ] Single CTA: clear, low-friction, limited choices, matched to trust level
- [ ] No spam trigger words
- [ ] No attachments (links only)
- [ ] Personalization: beyond first name, WITH relevance
- [ ] Verbe intime: using THEIR language, not yours
- [ ] Readability: 3rd-5th grade level
