# Segmentation Guide

## Core Philosophy [Moubeche principle]

"Only with Personas can you be sure you're talking to people with PROBABLY the same KPIs, or at least 90%+ the same."

Segment by pain point and business context — not just demographics. Demographic segments (industry, company size) are starting points. Pain-point segments are where conversion happens.

## Segmentation Hierarchy

### Level 1: Demographic (minimum viable)
- Industry / vertical
- Company size (employees or revenue)
- Role / job title
- Geography

### Level 2: Behavioral (better)
- Website pages visited
- Content consumed (which topics)
- Email engagement (opens, clicks, replies)
- Product usage (if applicable)
- Event attendance

### Level 3: Pain-Based (best) [Moubeche principle]
- Primary challenge they're trying to solve
- Trigger event that created urgency
- Current solution (or lack of)
- Buying stage / awareness level

## Practical Segmentation Matrix

| Segment | Definition | Email Approach | Cadence |
|---------|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **Cold ICP** | Matches ideal profile, no relationship | Cold outreach sequence | 5-7 emails / 15-25 days |
| **Warm subscriber** | Opted in, reading content | Nurture + education | 1-2x/week |
| **Hot lead** | High engagement signals | Sales sequence | More frequent, more direct |
| **Trial user** | Using product, not paying | Onboarding + conversion | Daily first week, then 2-3x/week |
| **Active customer** | Paying, using regularly | Retention tips + upsell | Weekly or bi-weekly |
| **At-risk** | Usage dropping | Churn prevention | Triggered, personalized |
| **Churned** | Cancelled / lapsed | Reactivation | 3-5 emails / 10-15 days |
| **Advocate** | Happy, refers others | Community + referral | Monthly, light touch |

## Signal-Based Targeting [Moubeche principle]

Instead of blasting entire lists, target based on signals:

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Recent funding round | Has budget, likely hiring | Outreach with growth angle |
| New job title | Decision-maker, open to change | Outreach with "new role" angle |
| Hiring for specific role | Investing in function | Outreach with efficiency angle |
| Competitor mention/review | Evaluating alternatives | Outreach with differentiation angle |
| Content engagement (your site) | Interested in topic | Move to targeted nurture |
| High email engagement | Warm, ready for next step | Escalate to sales sequence |
| Low engagement | Losing interest | Reactivation or list cleaning |

## Segmentation by Awareness Level [Inferred extension]

| Awareness | Messaging | Email Type |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Unaware | "Did you know [problem] costs companies [metric]?" | Educational cold outreach |
| Problem-Aware | "You're dealing with [problem]. Here's why it's getting worse." | Problem-agitation nurture |
| Solution-Aware | "Here's how [solution category] solves [problem]. We do it differently because..." | Comparison/differentiation |
| Product-Aware | "Here's why [our product] is the right fit for [your situation]." | Social proof + objection handling |
| Most Aware | "Ready? Here's the offer: [clear terms]." | Direct sales / cart close |

## Campaign Segmentation Rules [Moubeche principle]

- Each segment = dedicated campaign with tailored copy
- Segmented emails drive 30% more opens and 50% more click-throughs than unsegmented
- For cold outreach: max 100-200 prospects per segment/campaign
- Use lead scoring to prioritize follow-up on most engaged contacts
- Re-segment based on behavior, not just initial categorization

## Lead Scoring [Moubeche principle]

Assign points based on engagement:

| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Email opened | +1 |
| Link clicked | +3 |
| Email replied | +10 |
| Meeting booked | +20 |
| Website visit (from email) | +5 |
| Resource downloaded | +5 |
| Multiple emails opened | +2 per additional |
| No engagement after 3 emails | -5 |
| Unsubscribed | Remove from scoring |

**Thresholds:**
- Score > 30: Move to sales sequence
- Score 15-30: Continue nurture, increase frequency
- Score 5-15: Standard nurture cadence
- Score < 5: Reduce frequency or pause

## Segmentation Implementation Checklist

- [ ] Define 3-5 core segments based on pain points
- [ ] Tag all contacts with appropriate segment
- [ ] Create dedicated sequences per segment
- [ ] Set up behavioral triggers for automatic re-segmentation
- [ ] Implement lead scoring
- [ ] Review segment performance monthly
- [ ] Clean segments quarterly (remove dead contacts, update tags)
- [ ] Test messaging variations within segments
