# Email Rewrite & Optimization Framework

## The Rewrite Process

### Step 1: Diagnose Before Rewriting

NEVER rewrite an email without first diagnosing what's wrong. Use this matrix:

| What's Failing | Likely Problem | Where to Fix |
|----------------|---------------|--------------|
| Low opens (< 40%) | Subject line or deliverability | Subject line + technical check |
| Opens OK, no clicks | Body copy or CTA weakness | Body structure + CTA |
| Opens OK, no replies | Message-market mismatch or weak ask | Personalization + CTA + targeting |
| Clicks OK, no conversion | Landing page or offer problem | NOT an email problem |
| High unsubscribes | Wrong audience or too aggressive | Targeting + frequency + tone |
| Spam complaints | Content triggers or bad list | Technical + content review |

### Step 2: Identify the Specific Weakness

Run through this checklist on the existing email:

**Subject Line:**
- [ ] Is it 1-8 words?
- [ ] Does it create curiosity or name a pain?
- [ ] Is it free of spam trigger words?
- [ ] Would you open this if you received it?

**Opening Line:**
- [ ] Is it about the PROSPECT (not about you)?
- [ ] Does it demonstrate research or relevance?
- [ ] Does it avoid generic openers like "I hope this finds you well"?

**Body:**
- [ ] Is it under 125 words (cold) or appropriate length?
- [ ] Does it follow a framework (PAS, PIN, Proof-First)?
- [ ] Is the "you/your" ratio at least 2:1?
- [ ] Does it name a specific problem?
- [ ] Does it include proof (number, case study, name)?

**CTA:**
- [ ] Is there exactly ONE CTA?
- [ ] Does it match the current trust level?
- [ ] Is it under 7 words?
- [ ] Is it a low-friction ask?

**Overall:**
- [ ] Does every sentence earn its place?
- [ ] Is the reading level 3rd-5th grade?
- [ ] Would a busy person read this in 30 seconds?

### Step 3: Rewrite with Before/After

Always show:
1. The original email
2. What's wrong (specific diagnosis)
3. The rewritten version
4. Why each change was made

## Common Rewrite Patterns

### Pattern 1: Too Long → Ruthless Cut
**Problem:** Email is 250+ words, buries the point.
**Fix:** Cut to 100-120 words. Remove everything that doesn't serve the CTA.

**Before:**
```
Hi [Name],

I hope this email finds you well. My name is John and I work at XYZ Company.
We're a leading provider of sales automation software that helps businesses
streamline their outreach processes. We've been in business for 10 years and
serve over 5,000 customers worldwide. I noticed your company has been growing
rapidly and thought you might benefit from our platform. We offer features
like email automation, CRM integration, analytics dashboards, and team
collaboration tools. Many of our customers have seen a 40% increase in
their sales pipeline after implementing our solution. I'd love to schedule
a 30-minute demo to show you how we can help your team. Would you be
available next week? Please let me know what works for your schedule.

Best regards,
John
```

**After:**
```
Hey [Name],

Saw [Company] just expanded the sales team to 15 reps — congrats.

Quick question: how are you handling outreach at that scale?
Most teams I talk to hit a wall around 10 reps where manual
processes start costing pipeline.

We helped [Similar Company] recover 40% more pipeline by automating
the repetitive stuff without losing personalization.

Worth a 10-min look?
```

**Changes:** Removed self-introduction (nobody cares). Led with prospect context. Cut word count from 150+ to ~70. Single clear CTA. Added specific proof.

### Pattern 2: No Personalization → Tiramisu Treatment
**Problem:** Generic email that could be sent to anyone.
**Fix:** Add Tiramisu layers — specific prospect research in the intro + contextual pitch.

### Pattern 3: Multiple CTAs → Single Focus
**Problem:** "Book a demo, download our whitepaper, or follow us on LinkedIn!"
**Fix:** Pick the ONE action that matters most. Remove everything else.

### Pattern 4: Feature Dump → Pain-First
**Problem:** Lists product features without connecting to prospect's world.
**Fix:** Start with their pain, show impact, then position features as solutions.

### Pattern 5: Weak CTA → Binary Choice
**Problem:** "Let me know if you're interested" (vague, easy to ignore).
**Fix:** "This week or next?" or "Want me to send the 3 changes that worked for [company]?"

### Pattern 6: Self-Centered → Prospect-Centered
**Problem:** 80% of email talks about "we" and "our product."
**Fix:** Flip the ratio. 80% about them, their problems, their goals.

### Pattern 7: No Framework → Apply PAS
**Problem:** Stream-of-consciousness email with no structure.
**Fix:** Restructure around Problem → Agitate → Solution.

## A/B Testing Optimization [Moubeche principle]

### What to Test (in priority order):
1. **Subject lines** — highest impact on opens
2. **Opening lines** — highest impact on read-through
3. **CTA format** — highest impact on reply/click
4. **Body copy angle** — pain point A vs pain point B
5. **Email length** — shorter vs longer
6. **Tone** — formal vs casual
7. **Personalization depth** — basic vs deep
8. **Send time** — morning vs afternoon (lower impact)

### Testing Rules [Moubeche principle]:
- Minimum 100 prospects per variation
- Test ONE variable at a time
- Test messaging first (10x more impact than timing/structure)
- Run for at least 1 full sequence cycle before judging
- Track reply rate as primary metric, not just opens

### Testing Template:
```
## A/B Test: [What you're testing]
**Hypothesis:** [Version A will outperform B because...]
**Variable:** [Exact change between A and B]
**Sample size:** [X prospects per version]
**Duration:** [X days]
**Primary metric:** [Reply rate / Open rate / Click rate]
**Result:** [Pending]
**Winner:** [A / B]
**Learning:** [What this teaches us]
**Next test:** [What to test next based on this result]
```

## Subject Line Optimization [Moubeche principle]

### Generation Process:
When asked to write subject lines, generate 10+ options across these angles:

| Angle | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Pain-focused | "{{painPoint}} getting worse?" |
| Curiosity | "quick question about [topic]" |
| Social proof | "[competitor] just did this" |
| Trigger-based | "congrats on the funding" |
| Direct | "idea for {{company}}" |
| Question | "struggling with [problem]?" |
| Result-focused | "40% more pipeline" |
| Contrarian | "stop doing [common practice]" |
| Personal | "{{firstName}}, saw your post" |
| Referral | "[mutual connection] suggested I reach out" |

### Subject Line Rules [Moubeche principle]:
- 1-8 words (sweet spot: 1-5 words)
- Lowercase preferred for cold outreach
- No spam triggers (FREE, GUARANTEED, ACT NOW)
- No ALL CAPS
- 0-1 exclamation marks max
- Personalization variables improve relevance but {{firstName}} alone has negligible impact
