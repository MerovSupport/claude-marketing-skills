# Sequence Blueprints

## Sequence Selection Matrix

| Goal | Sequence Type | Length | Timing |
|------|--------------|--------|--------|
| Book meetings with cold prospects | Cold Outreach | 5-7 emails + multichannel | 15-25 days |
| Onboard new signups/customers | Welcome/Onboarding | 5-8 emails | 14-21 days |
| Build trust with warm leads | Nurture | 6-12 emails | 30-60 days |
| Close a specific offer | Sales | 4-7 emails | 7-14 days |
| Launch a new product/feature | Launch | 5-9 emails | 10-14 days |
| Run evergreen conversion | Evergreen | 7-14 emails | 30-90 days |
| Re-engage inactive contacts | Reactivation | 3-5 emails | 10-15 days |
| Prevent churn | Retention | 3-5 emails | Triggered |
| Follow up no-shows/abandoned | Recovery | 3-4 emails | 3-7 days |
| Convert newsletter readers | Newsletter → Sale | Ongoing + trigger | Varies |

## Blueprint 1: Cold Outreach — Full Multichannel Sequence [Moubeche principle]

### The Moubeche 9-Step Multichannel Sequence (45 days total)

This is the actual operational sequence from Guillaume's videos. The 3-week familiarity period is NOT optional — it's the highest-leverage part.

**PHASE 0 — FAMILIARITY BUILDING (Day -21 to Day 0)**

```
Day -21: LinkedIn connection request
  → NO message in the invitation
  → Your LinkedIn profile must speak to their problems
  → Example headline: "I share daily tips on scaling B2B outbound"

Days -21 to 0: DO NOTHING — Let familiarity build
  → They see your posts in their feed
  → They visit your profile
  → "À partir du moment où tu as de la familiarité avec quelqu'un,
     quand cette personne t'écrit, tu es plus à même de répondre"
```

**PHASE 1 — OUTREACH (Day 0 to Day 25)**

**Step 1 — Email 1 (Day 0)**
- Tiramisu personalization WITH relevance bridge
- PAS framework or Value-First approach
- About THEM, not about you
- Pattern interrupt opening (no "Bonjour prénom")
- Single soft CTA
- 75-120 words

**Step 2 — Email 2 (Day 2)**
- Same thread (no new subject line)
- VERY SHORT: "Juste un petit message pour savoir si tu avais bien reçu mon email ?"
- One line. Low effort to respond.
- Can add a specific time CTA: "dispo jeudi ?"
- 20-50 words

**Step 3 — LinkedIn Message 1 (Day 5)**
- Puts you in direct contact
- They can click your profile and see your content
- Short, value-oriented, references your email
- 50-80 words

**Step 4 — LinkedIn Follow-Up (Day 7)**
- Brief follow-up on LinkedIn
- New angle or quick value add
- 30-50 words

**Step 5 — LinkedIn Profile Visit (Day 9)**
- Just visit their profile
- Creates a notification
- Passive reminder of your existence

**Step 6 — Email 3 (Day 12)**
- Mention you also reached out on LinkedIn
- Add case studies or social proof (success stories from similar clients)
- More value, slightly more direct
- 80-120 words

**Step 7 — Email 4 (Day 16)**
- Share content: article, guide, insight
- "Some prospects won't talk to you until they've consumed your content"
- Position as expert, not seller
- 80-120 words

**Step 8 — Email 5 or LinkedIn (Day 20)**
- Direct ask or breakup approach
- Restate core value in one sentence
- Calendar link or specific time
- 50-80 words

**Step 9 — Final Touch (Day 25)**
- Breakup email OR referral ask
- "Est-ce que tu connais d'autres personnes qui ont le problème XYZ ?"
- Permission to close
- 50-80 words

### Timing Variation Rules [Moubeche principle]

> "Alterner les jours. 7 jours exactement, ça fait trop automatisé. Les gens se disent 'tous les jeudis, il m'envoie le même message.'"

- Vary gaps between 2-8 days (never exactly the same interval)
- Vary days of the week
- You don't know their schedule — "le jeudi c'est peut-être le jour où il fait de l'équitation"

### The 3-Tier Personalization System [Moubeche principle]

| Tier | Effort | Personalization | Volume |
|------|--------|----------------|--------|
| **Tier 1** (highest value) | 5-10 min/prospect | Manual icebreaker: read their articles, watch content, find specific connection. "J'ai adoré ton article sur X, j'ai bien aimé quand tu parlais de tes erreurs en recrutement" | Low volume, high conversion |
| **Tier 2** (medium value) | 1-2 min/prospect | Segment-based: "I saw you sell courses + have a YouTube channel with lots of subscribers" — applies to many but feels personal | Medium volume |
| **Tier 3** (lower value) | Automated | Variable-based: industry, role, company size, trigger events | High volume, lower conversion |

### Image & Video Personalization [Moubeche principle]

| Format | Description | Effect |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Starbucks cup** | Your photo holding a coffee cup with {{firstName}} dynamically inserted | "Le premier truc que tu vois, c'est un mec avec un gobelet avec ton prénom" |
| **Website screenshot** | Your photo at laptop with {{websiteScreenshot}} dynamically inserted | "On était en train de regarder ton site internet et on a noté 5-6 idées" |
| **Personalized video** (Tier 1) | < 1 minute video about the specific person | "Faire des recherches, dire pourquoi on devrait se rencontrer" |
| **Segment video** (Tier 2) | < 1 minute video for a target segment | "Describe them in 2-3 lines. They feel touched even though many people match the description." |

### Performance Benchmarks [Moubeche principle]
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | > 50% |
| Reply rate (cumulative) | > 8-15% |
| Positive reply rate | > 5% |
| Booking rate | > 3% of total sent |
| Bounce rate | < 2% |

## Blueprint 2: Welcome / Onboarding Sequence [Inferred extension]

Based on Moubeche's Growth Circle of Love (Inspire → Educate → Convert → Retain) and his philosophy of exceptional onboarding.

### Structure (5-8 emails, 14-21 days)

**Email 1 — Welcome + Quick Win (Immediate)**
- Warm welcome, set expectations
- One specific action they can take RIGHT NOW
- Link to the single most important feature/resource
- Establish tone and personality
- 150-200 words

**Email 2 — The "Why" (Day 1)**
- Why they made the right choice
- One customer success story (short)
- Core value proposition reinforced
- Next step to explore
- 150-200 words

**Email 3 — First Milestone (Day 3)**
- Guide them to their first meaningful result
- Step-by-step walkthrough (3-5 steps max)
- "Reply if you're stuck" CTA (opens conversation)
- 150-250 words

**Email 4 — Education (Day 5)**
- Teach one key concept or best practice
- Link to resource (video, guide, template)
- Position as expert partner, not vendor
- 150-200 words

**Email 5 — Social Proof (Day 7)**
- Feature a user who achieved results
- Specific numbers and timeline
- "Here's how they did it" breakdown
- CTA: try the same approach
- 150-250 words

**Email 6 — Overcome Friction (Day 10)**
- Address the #1 reason people don't activate
- Anticipate objections/confusion
- Offer help (reply, chat, call)
- 100-150 words

**Email 7 — Upgrade/Convert (Day 14)**
- If freemium: present the upgrade case
- If trial: remind of deadline + value received
- If paid: suggest next-level usage
- One clear CTA
- 100-200 words

**Email 8 — Community (Day 21)**
- Invite to community/group
- Introduce other resources
- Set up ongoing relationship
- 100-150 words

## Blueprint 3: Nurture Sequence [Inferred extension, based on Growth Circle of Love]

### Structure (6-12 emails, 30-60 days)

**Pattern: Value-Value-Value-Soft Ask (repeat)**

| Email | Type | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Inspire | Story or insight that shifts their thinking |
| 2 | Educate | Actionable tactic they can use today |
| 3 | Educate | Framework or template with real examples |
| 4 | Soft sell | Case study + "want similar results?" |
| 5 | Inspire | Industry insight or contrarian take |
| 6 | Educate | Common mistake + how to fix it |
| 7 | Social proof | Customer story with numbers |
| 8 | Sell | Direct offer with clear CTA |
| 9 | Educate | Advanced tactic or resource |
| 10 | Inspire | Vision/mission-aligned content |
| 11 | Educate | FAQ / objection handling |
| 12 | Sell | Final push with urgency or bonus |

**Ratio:** 3:1 (value : ask). Never sell in consecutive emails.

**Timing:** Every 3-5 days for engaged leads. Every 7-10 days for warm-but-passive leads.

## Blueprint 4: Sales Sequence [Moubeche principle + Inferred extension]

### Structure (4-7 emails, 7-14 days)

**Email 1 — The Offer (Day 0)**
- Clear, direct offer statement
- Who it's for and who it's NOT for
- Key benefit (one sentence)
- Social proof (one line)
- CTA: learn more or buy
- 150-200 words

**Email 2 — The Proof (Day 2)**
- Customer case study with specific numbers
- Before/after transformation
- CTA: same offer
- 150-250 words

**Email 3 — The Objection Killer (Day 4)**
- Address #1 objection head-on
- Use their language, not yours
- Reframe the risk
- CTA: same offer
- 150-200 words

**Email 4 — The Education (Day 6)**
- Teach something valuable related to the offer
- Show expertise without selling
- Subtle connection to the offer
- 200-300 words

**Email 5 — The Stack (Day 8)**
- List everything they get
- Anchor value vs price
- Guarantee or risk reversal
- CTA: buy/signup
- 200-300 words

**Email 6 — The Urgency (Day 11)**
- Real deadline or scarcity (never fake)
- Recap core benefit
- Last-chance framing
- CTA: buy now
- 100-150 words

**Email 7 — The Close (Day 14)**
- Final email
- Emotional + logical appeal
- Summary of transformation
- Clear deadline
- 100-200 words

| Phase | Emails | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|

## Blueprint 6: Reactivation Sequence [Inferred extension]

### Structure (3-5 emails, 10-15 days)

**Email 1 — The Check-In (Day 0)**
- Acknowledge the gap ("It's been a while")
- No guilt. No pressure.
- Share one new thing that's changed/improved
- Soft CTA: "Still interested in [topic]?"
- 80-120 words

**Email 2 — The Value Gift (Day 3)**
- Give them something useful with zero ask
- Resource, template, insight, tool
- "Thought this might help regardless"
- 80-120 words

**Email 3 — The Social Proof (Day 7)**
- Share a recent result or success story
- Imply they're missing out (without saying it)
- CTA: re-engage or reply
- 80-120 words

**Email 4 — The Direct Question (Day 10)**
- "Should I keep sending you [topic] content?"
- Yes/No binary CTA
- Segment based on response
- 50-80 words

**Email 5 — The Breakup (Day 15)**
- "I'm going to remove you from this list"
- Last chance to stay
- Clean, respectful, no guilt
- 50-80 words

## Blueprint 7: Retention / Churn Prevention [Moubeche principle: reduce churn by acquiring the right customers]

### Trigger-Based Emails

| Trigger | Email |
|---------|-------|
| No login in 7 days | "Quick tip to get more from [product]" |
| Usage drop > 50% | "We noticed you haven't used [feature] lately" |
| Support ticket unresolved | "Following up on your issue" |
| Approaching renewal | Value recap + renewal reminder |
| Cancel page visited | Intercept: "Before you go — can we help?" |
| Actually cancelled | Exit survey + door-open message |
| 30 days post-cancel | Win-back: what's improved since they left |

## Blueprint 8: Abandoned Cart / No-Show / Recovery [Inferred extension]

### No-Show After Booked Meeting (3 emails)

**Email 1 (1 hour after):**
"Hey [name], looks like we missed each other. No worries — here's a fresh link to rebook: [calendar]"

**Email 2 (Day 2):**
"Still interested in exploring [topic]? Happy to adjust timing. Alternatively, I can send a quick [video/overview] if a call isn't ideal right now."

**Email 3 (Day 5):**
"I know things get busy. If [problem] is still on your radar, I'm here. If not, totally fine — just let me know and I'll stop following up."

### Abandoned Cart (3 emails) [Inferred extension]

**Email 1 (1 hour):** Reminder + address likely friction point
**Email 2 (24 hours):** Social proof + FAQ
**Email 3 (48-72 hours):** Final reminder + small incentive or urgency
