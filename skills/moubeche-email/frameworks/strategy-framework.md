# Email Strategy & Lifecycle Framework

## The Growth Circle of Love [Moubeche principle]

Guillaume Moubeche's proprietary growth funnel:

```
INSPIRE → EDUCATE → CONVERT → RETAIN
   ↑                                |
   └────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Inspire:** Create content that sparks interest and shifts thinking.
**Educate:** Teach actionable tactics to those who don't know how to start.
**Convert:** Use built trust to demonstrate product/offer value.
**Retain:** Help customers be as successful as possible (they become advocates who inspire others).

Every email in every sequence should map to one of these four stages.

## Awareness Level Mapping [Inferred extension]

Based on Eugene Schwartz's awareness levels, adapted for email with Moubeche's trust-first philosophy:

| Level | Prospect Knows | Email Approach | Trust Required |
|-------|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Unaware** | Nothing about the problem | Inspire — shock, educate, reframe | Lowest |
| **Problem-Aware** | They have a problem, not solutions | Agitate pain, name the problem specifically | Low |
| **Solution-Aware** | Solutions exist, not yours | Differentiate, proof, comparison | Medium |
| **Product-Aware** | Your solution, undecided | Handle objections, social proof, case studies | Medium-High |
| **Most Aware** | Ready to buy, needs a push | Direct offer, urgency, terms, guarantee | Highest |

### How This Maps to Email Types:

| Awareness | Best Sequence Type | Lead Source |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Unaware | Cold outreach + content | Bought lists, scraped |
| Problem-Aware | Nurture sequence | Blog readers, webinar attendees |
| Solution-Aware | Comparison/education sequence | Competitors' audiences |
| Product-Aware | Sales sequence | Trial users, demo attendees |
| Most Aware | Direct offer / cart close | Pricing page visitors, repeat buyers |

## Strategy Design Process

When building an email strategy from scratch:

### Step 1: Map the Current State
- What emails are being sent today?
- What's the customer journey from first touch to purchase?
- Where are the gaps?
- What metrics exist?

### Step 2: Identify the Biggest Lever
Apply the Moubeche diagnostic: [Moubeche principle]
- If you have traffic but no leads → need better lead capture
- If you have leads but no sales → need better nurture/sales sequence
- If you have sales but high churn → need better onboarding/retention
- If you have nothing → start with cold outreach

### Step 3: Build the Foundation
Priority order:
1. **Deliverability setup** (SPF/DKIM/DMARC, warm-up)
2. **Welcome/onboarding sequence** (capture new signups immediately)
3. **Core conversion sequence** (the emails that make money)
4. **Nurture sequence** (for leads not ready to buy)
5. **Retention emails** (keep customers engaged)
6. **Reactivation** (recover lost leads/customers)

### Step 4: Connect to Content [Moubeche principle]
Content amplifies email performance 2-3x.
- LinkedIn posts → email topics
- Blog articles → nurture content
- Customer stories → social proof emails
- Webinars → conversion events
- Community discussions → email angles

### Step 5: Measure and Iterate
Track by sequence, not just overall:
| Metric | Frequency | Action Threshold |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Reply rate by email | Weekly | < 3% = rewrite |
| Open rate by sequence | Weekly | < 40% = deliverability check |
| Conversion by sequence | Monthly | Below target = audit |
| Unsubscribe rate | Weekly | > 5% = targeting review |
| Revenue per email sent | Monthly | Declining = strategy review |

## Segmentation Strategy [Moubeche principle + Inferred extension]

### Moubeche's Approach: Segment by Pain Point [Moubeche principle]
"Only with Personas can you be sure you're talking to people with probably the same KPIs."

Don't segment by demographics alone. Segment by:
1. **Primary pain point** — what problem are they trying to solve?
2. **Awareness level** — how much do they know about solutions?
3. **Engagement level** — how actively are they interacting with you?
4. **Buying stage** — where are they in the decision process?

### Practical Segmentation Matrix [Inferred extension]

| Segment | Definition | Email Treatment |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Cold ICP | Matches profile, no relationship | Cold outreach sequence |
| Warm subscriber | Opted in, consuming content | Nurture sequence |
| Engaged lead | High open/click, replies | Sales sequence |
| Trial user | Using product, not paying | Onboarding + conversion |
| Active customer | Paying, using regularly | Retention + upsell |
| At-risk customer | Usage declining | Churn prevention |
| Churned | Cancelled or lapsed | Reactivation |
| Advocate | Happy, vocal, refers | Community + referral |

## Newsletter → Conversion Logic [Moubeche principle: AIDA framework]

### Newsletter Structure (AIDA) [Moubeche principle]
- **Attention**: Captivating fact, story, or hook
- **Interest**: Connect to reader's specific goals
- **Desire**: Real-life success story or proof
- **Action**: Clear implementation step

### Newsletter Conversion Principles [Inferred extension]
1. Newsletters build trust over time — they are NOT sales emails
2. Ratio: 80% value, 20% soft promotion (mention, not pitch)
3. Include ONE clear CTA per newsletter (not multiple offers)
4. Use newsletters to segment: track clicks by topic → build segments
5. Monthly "full send" sales email is acceptable if value ratio is maintained
6. Convert newsletter readers by moving them into targeted sequences based on behavior

### Newsletter → Sales Pipeline [Inferred extension]
```
Newsletter reader clicks [topic X] link
→ Tagged as interested in [topic X]
→ Enters targeted nurture sequence for [topic X]
→ Receives relevant case study
→ CTA to book call / start trial / buy
```

## Email-to-Funnel Consistency [Inferred extension]

Every email must be consistent with:
1. **The landing page it links to** — same language, same offer, same design tone
2. **The previous email in the sequence** — logical progression, not random
3. **The brand voice** — consistent personality across all touchpoints
4. **The trust level** — don't ask for more trust than you've earned
5. **The awareness level** — don't assume knowledge they don't have

### Consistency Checklist:
- [ ] Email promise matches landing page headline
- [ ] CTA language is identical in email and button on page
- [ ] Offer terms in email match offer terms on page
- [ ] Tone in email matches tone on page
- [ ] If email mentions a specific benefit, page reinforces it above the fold
