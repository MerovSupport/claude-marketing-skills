# Subject Line Guide

## Core Rules [Moubeche principle]

1. **1-8 words maximum.** Sweet spot is 1-5 words.
2. **Casual tone.** Write like you'd text a colleague.
3. **3rd grade readability.** Simple enough for a child to understand.
4. **0-1 exclamation marks.** More = declining performance.
5. **No spam trigger words.** See banned list below.
6. **No ALL CAPS.** Ever.
7. **Lowercase preferred** for cold outreach (feels personal, not mass-sent).
8. **Pain > benefit.** "Negative reviews on Google" outperforms "Google presence insights."

## Data Points [Moubeche principle]

- Open rates drop 25% when subject lines exceed 13 words
- {{firstName}} alone shows "no notable difference" in open rates
- {{painPoint}} and {{competitor}} variables outperform name-only personalization
- Readability alone increases open rates by 6%

## Subject Line Angles (with templates)

### 1. Pain-Focused
```
{{painPoint}} getting worse?
struggling with [problem]?
[problem] at {{company}}?
losing [metric] to [cause]?
```

### 2. Curiosity / Open Loop
```
quick question
one idea for {{company}}
thought about this
noticed something
```

### 3. Social Proof
```
[competitor] just switched
how [similar company] fixed [problem]
what [industry] leaders are doing differently
```

### 4. Trigger-Based
```
congrats on the funding
saw your post about [topic]
re: {{company}}'s new [initiative]
your [recent event]
```

### 5. Direct / Simple
```
idea for {{company}}
[topic]
{{firstName}} — quick thought
can I help?
```

### 6. Question
```
still using [old method]?
who handles [function] at {{company}}?
open to a quick chat?
```

### 7. Result-Focused
```
40% more [metric]
cut [problem] in half
[company] went from X to Y
```

### 8. Contrarian
```
stop doing [common practice]
[popular advice] is wrong
why [accepted truth] fails
```

### 9. Personal Connection
```
{{firstName}}, loved your take on [topic]
fellow [industry/role] here
[mutual connection] mentioned you
```

### 10. Urgency (use sparingly, never fake)
```
last call — [offer]
closing [date]
{{firstName}}, before Friday
```

## High-Performing Examples [Moubeche data]

These achieved 80%+ open rates in lemlist campaigns:
1. "Loved your last article!"
2. "Last call {{firstname}}... closing soon"
3. "Here's a little surprise for you {{firstName}}!"
4. "Collaboration with {{Domain}}"
5. "{{firstName}}, how about we have a chat?"
6. "3 ideas for {{painPoint}}"
7. "Question about company culture @ {{companyname}}"
8. "Hey {{firstName}}, congrats on your funding round"

## Banned Words [Moubeche principle]

Never use in subject lines:
Congratulations! | 100% Free | No fees | Win | Click now | Make money | LIMITED TIME! | Act now | Call now | You're a winner | Not spam | Get paid | No cost | Work from home | Get started free | Increase sales | About to expire | Unlimited | Join millions | Satisfaction guaranteed

## Follow-Up Subject Lines [Moubeche principle]

**Rule:** Do NOT add a subject line to follow-up emails. Keep all follow-ups in the same thread as Email 1. Only the first email gets a subject line.

Exception: If starting a completely new angle (rare), a new subject line is acceptable.

## Subject Line Generation Process

When asked to generate subject lines:

1. Ask: What's the email type? (cold, nurture, sales, launch, reactivation)
2. Ask: Who's the audience? (role, industry, pain point)
3. Ask: What's the core offer/value?
4. Generate 10-15 options across at least 5 different angles
5. Rank by likely performance with reasoning
6. Recommend top 2-3 for A/B testing
7. Flag any that risk spam filters

## Preview Text [Inferred extension]

Preview text (preheader) appears after the subject line in the inbox. Rules:
- 40-130 characters
- Must complement the subject line (not repeat it)
- Should add context or intrigue that the subject line opened
- Think of it as the "second hook"

| Subject Line | Bad Preview | Good Preview |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| "quick question" | "Hi, I wanted to reach out..." | "about your team's outreach process" |
| "idea for {{company}}" | "My name is John and I work at..." | "saw something in your funnel worth fixing" |
| "{{painPoint}} getting worse?" | "We offer solutions for..." | "here's what [similar company] did about it" |
