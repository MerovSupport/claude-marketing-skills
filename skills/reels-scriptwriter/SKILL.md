## PART 1 — STRATEGIC FOUNDATION

### Core Insight (from 2.3M+ impressions analyzed)

**Content does NOT perform because it's informative. It performs because it creates a personal threat + a solution.**

The data proves it:
- "Loi/liberte/controle" content generates **3-8x more engagement** than pure crypto content
- People share when they feel **personally threatened**, not when told to buy Bitcoin
- The #1 hook pattern ("info cachee / personne n'en parle") averages **175K impressions** — 3x the average
- Best engagement rate ever: **11.4%** on sovereignty/censorship content

### The Hormozi Layer — Pain > Information

Every script must answer these questions BEFORE writing:
1. **What pain does this address?** (financial loss, missing out, being controlled, ignorance)
2. **What desire does this activate?** (protection, freedom, wealth, being ahead)
3. **What belief must shift?** (the system is fair → the system is rigged, crypto is gambling → crypto is protection)
4. **What is the dream outcome?** (financial sovereignty, being on the right side, protecting family)
5. **What is the perceived likelihood of achievement?** (proof through data, historical patterns, institutional behavior)

**Hormozi formula applied to Reels:**
- Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
- In 60 seconds: maximize dream outcome and perceived likelihood, minimize perceived effort
- Every script needs: a clear problem, an escalation, a personal stake, and a path forward

### The Roro Vevo Layer — How to SOUND

These patterns come from analyzing a French crypto creator who buzzes consistently. The scripts must SOUND like someone talking, not reading.

**Vocal patterns to embed:**
- **"Je m'explique."** — natural transition to context, not scolaire
- **"Aussi simple que ca."** — simplifies complex ideas into one confident sentence
- **"Moi c'est [action]. Apres c'est toi qui vois."** — personal positioning, not preachy
- **"C'est toujours le meme schema."** — pattern recognition = authority
- **"Faut que tu comprennes un truc."** — pulls the listener in like a WhatsApp voice note
- **"Et le pire c'est que..."** — escalation connector
- **"C'est comme si..."** — analogy bridge for vulgarization
- **Rhetorical questions embedded** — "A qui profite le crime ?"
- **Story hooks** — open with a personal moment or scenario, not a fact
- **Vulnerability** — "moi le premier, je pourrais me tromper" — admission = trust

**What to AVOID sounding like:**
- "Aujourd'hui on va parler de..." (educational, kills retention)
- "Salut, bienvenue..." (generic, skip-inducing)
- "Il faut savoir que..." (formal, academic)
- "Les gars" every sentence (forced familiarity)
- Long subordinate clauses (spoken French = short phrases)

## PART 4 — SCRIPT STRUCTURE

### The 5-Act Formula (every top performer follows this)

```
ACT 1 — HOOK (0-3s)
   Short, affirmative, shocking phrase.
   = The overlay text.
   MAX 8 words.

ACT 2 — CONTEXT (3-15s)
   Set the scene like a STORY, not a lesson.
   "Faut que tu comprennes un truc." / "Je m'explique."
   Vulgarize the key concept so even a beginner follows.
   If possible, anchor with a temporal comparison (past vs now).
   Ex: "En 2022, il s'est passe exactement la meme chose..."

ACT 3 — ESCALATION (15-45s)
   NARRATE, don't list. Facts must CHAIN narratively, not stack as bullets.
   Domino effect: A causes B, B causes C, C affects YOU.
   Each fact connects to the previous with a narrative bridge.
   Precise numbers at each step.
   TAKE A CLEAR POSITION — don't stay neutral. Say what you think.
   "Tu vois ou je veux en venir?" / "Et c'est la que ca devient interessant"
   "Et c'est pas tout." / "Et le pire..." / "Mais attends."
   BAD: "Indicateur 1 dit X. Indicateur 2 dit Y. Indicateur 3 dit Z."
   GOOD: "En 2022, X s'est passe. Aujourd'hui, rebelote. Et le pire c'est que Y..."

ACT 4 — PERSONAL IMPACT (45-55s)
   Go from macro to micro, from the world to YOU.
   "Si t'as un PEA / Si t'as 25 ans / Si t'as de l'epargne..."
   The viewer must feel PERSONALLY concerned.
   The story must land on THEM — not float in abstract analysis.

ACT 5 — CLOSER + CTA (55-60s)
   Strong statement OR sticky personal question.
   + CTA if applicable ("commente 'strategie'" / "abonne-toi")
```

### Proven Templates

**Template 1: "Info Cachee" (#1 performing pattern)**
```
Hook "personne n'en parle" → Context simplified → Escalation of facts → Personal impact → CTA
```
Best for: Laws, institutional moves, hidden decisions

**Template 2: "Deadline Menace"**
```
"Dans X jours, [event]" → What changes → 3 escalating consequences → The real threat behind → Solution
```
Best for: Regulatory deadlines, policy changes

**Template 3: "Crash + Opportunite"**
```
"[Asset] vient de perdre X%" → Panic context → Reversal (what big money does) → Data → "Last time this happened, x[N]"
```
Best for: Market dips, historical patterns, buying opportunities

**Template 4: "Institution fait [chose choc]"**
```
"[BlackRock] vient de [unexpected action]" → They said the opposite → Implications → What you need to do
```
Best for: Institutional adoption, regulatory shifts

**Template 5: "Pattern Historique"**
```
"[Old person/method] predicts [current thing]" → Simple explanation → Stacked historical proofs → Application now → Open question
```
Best for: Evergreen education with dramatic angle

**Template 6: "Contrarian"**
```
"[Popular belief] — c'est faux." → Why everyone believes it → The real data → What it actually means → "Aussi simple que ca."
```
Best for: Mythbusting, audience differentiation, high engagement

**Template 7: "Comparaison Temporelle" (narrative-driven, works for long scripts too)**
```
"Le marche repete exactement le meme film qu'en [ANNEE]" → Point par point: voila ce qui s'est passe a l'epoque → Aujourd'hui, rebelote → La suite logique si le pattern continue → "Tu vois ou je veux en venir?"
```
Best for: Market cycles, historical patterns, macro analysis. Works with long-form (1:30+) because the viewer FOLLOWS a story, not a list. Key: take a clear position on what happens next.

## PART 5 — WRITING RULES

### Tone & Style (non-negotiable)
- **Conversational**, never formal or academic
- **Short sentences.** No complex subordinate clauses.
- The viewer must understand without mental pause
- **Analogies from daily life** to explain complex concepts
- Scripts are SPOKEN — write how people talk, not how people write
- Use natural transitions: "Je m'explique", "En gros", "Aussi simple que ca", "C'est comme si"

### Formatting (for prompter reading)
- **OVERLAY:** as first line if overlay text is planned
- Line break every 1-2 sentences
- Empty line between logical beats (hook / context / escalation / impact)
- NO numbering
- NO headers in the script
- NO "[FIN]" at the end
- NO "Duree estimee: XX secondes"
- NO stage directions in parentheses
- NO emojis in the script itself (only in covers/overlays)

### Content Rules
- **NEVER invent** numbers, testimonials, or news — ask if you don't have them
- **Mandatory disclaimer** on any content with financial results
- **Sources:** Always cite where info comes from (law, report, tweet, article)
- **Tu/ton/tes** mandatory (NEVER vous/votre) for all brands
- Explain institutions and people (for French beginners)
- Vocabulary adapted to persona (see Part 2)

### CTA Rules
- CTA = "Commente 'strategie'" or "Abonne-toi" + short reason
- NEVER CTA about leverage, trading, or technical analysis
- CTA must flow naturally after the closer, not feel forced
- Not every script needs a hard CTA — some end on a strong statement

### Cross-Post Rules
- Tanguy posts FIRST (2-4h ahead)
- NO identical cross-post on IG between brands (vary hook/audio/caption)
- MAX 2 reels/day on IG for Tanguy
- 3 exclusive Tanguy reels/week minimum
- When same script across brands: change hook, CTA, and caption for each

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|

- At least 1 "screenshot-worthy" phrase (shareable out of context)
